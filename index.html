☀️ The Sun

Our journey begins at the very center of our solar system... the Sun. As you may already know, the Sun is a massive star that provides light and heat to makes life on Earth possible. Without the sun, we wouldn't even be alive!

The Sun is so large that about 1.3 million Earths could fit inside it. It's made mostly of hydrogen and helium, and its core reaches temperatures of 15 million degrees Celsius!

Sun Facts

Age: 4.6 billion years
Surface Temperature: 5,500°C
Distance from Earth: 150 million km
Diameter: 1.39 million km

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system. It is also the closest to the Sun! However, despite being so close to the Sun, it's not the hottest planet (continue on your journey to find out which planet is!)

A day on Mercury/one rotation takes about 59 Earth days, but a year on Mercury/one orbit around the Sun takes only 88 Earth days. This means that a day on Mercury is longer than a year!

Mercury Facts

Distance from Sun: ~57.9 million km
Diameter: 4,890 km
Age: 4.503 billion years
Temperature Range: -173°C to 427°C
