Our journey begins at the center of our solar system - the Sun! This massive star provides the light and heat that makes life on Earth possible.

The Sun is so large that about 1.3 million Earths could fit inside it. It's made mostly of hydrogen and helium, and its core reaches temperatures of 15 million degrees Celsius!

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun. Despite being so close to our star, it's not the hottest planet - that honor goes to Venus!

A day on Mercury (one rotation) takes 59 Earth days, but a year (one orbit around the Sun) takes only 88 Earth days. This means Mercury's day is longer than its year!

Mercury Facts

Distance from Sun: 58 million km
Diameter: 4,879 km
Day Length: 59 Earth days
Temperature Range: -173¬∞C to 427¬∞C

Venus

Venus is often called Earth's "twin" because of its similar size, but it's actually more like Earth's evil twin! It's the hottest planet in our solar system.

The thick atmosphere traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect, making Venus hotter than Mercury. It also rains sulfuric acid and has crushing atmospheric pressure!

Venus Facts

Distance from Sun: 108 million km
Diameter: 12,104 km
Surface Temperature: 462¬∞C
Atmospheric Pressure: 92x Earth's

Home sweet home! Earth is the only planet we know of that supports life. It has liquid water, a protective atmosphere, and the perfect distance from the Sun.

Earth is about 71% covered in water and has one natural satellite - the Moon. Our planet's magnetic field protects us from harmful solar radiation.

Earth Facts

Distance from Sun: 150 million km
Diameter: 12,756 km
Day Length: 24 hours
Moons: 1 (The Moon)

Mars

The Red Planet! Mars gets its reddish color from iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It has the largest volcano in the solar system - Olympus Mons!

Mars has polar ice caps, seasonal weather changes, and evidence suggests it once had flowing water. It's a prime target for future human exploration!

Mars Facts

Distance from Sun: 228 million km
Diameter: 6,792 km
Day Length: 24.6 hours
Moons: 2 (Phobos & Deimos)

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system - so big that all other planets could fit inside it! It's a gas giant with a famous Great Red Spot storm.

This massive planet acts like a cosmic vacuum cleaner, protecting inner planets from asteroids and comets. It has at least 79 known moons, including the four largest discovered by Galileo!

Jupiter Facts

Distance from Sun: 778 million km
Diameter: 142,984 km
Day Length: 9.9 hours
Known Moons: 79+

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its spectacular ring system! These rings are made of billions of ice and rock particles, some as small as grains of sand, others as large as mountains.

Saturn is less dense than water - if you could find an ocean big enough, Saturn would float! It's also known for its moon Titan, which has lakes of liquid methane.

Saturn Facts

Distance from Sun: 1.4 billion km
Diameter: 120,536 km
Day Length: 10.7 hours
Known Moons: 82+

Uranus

Uranus is the "sideways planet" - it rotates on its side! This ice giant is made mostly of water, methane, and ammonia ice around a rocky core.

It's the coldest planet in our solar system, even though Neptune is farther from the Sun. Uranus also has faint rings and 27 known moons named after Shakespeare characters!

Uranus Facts

Distance from Sun: 2.9 billion km
Diameter: 51,118 km
Day Length: 17.2 hours
Temperature: -195¬∞C

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and the windiest! Winds can reach speeds of up to 2,100 kilometers per hour - faster than the speed of sound on Earth!

This deep blue ice giant takes 165 Earth years to orbit the Sun once. It was discovered through mathematical predictions rather than observation!

Neptune Facts

Distance from Sun: 4.5 billion km
Diameter: 49,528 km
Day Length: 16.1 hours
Wind Speed: Up to 2,100 km/h

The Age of Our Solar System

Our solar system formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing cloud of gas and dust

Planet Types

We have 4 rocky inner planets (terrestrial) and 4 gas/ice giants in the outer system

Natural Satellites

There are over 200 known moons orbiting planets in our solar system!

Other Objects

Our solar system also contains asteroids, comets, meteoroids, and dwarf planets like Pluto

Habitable Zone

Earth sits in the "Goldilocks Zone" which isn't too hot or too cold, it's just right for liquid water!

Exploration

Humans have sent robotic missions to every planet and even landed on Mars, Venus, and Titan!
